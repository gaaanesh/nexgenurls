How to become a Graphical Designer in India

Ankit Agrawal - Thursday 8th September 2022

Career in Graphical Designing

Are you a creative person? Do you enjoy designing creative visual content? Do you enjoy making creative content using computer software? If yes, you should consider graphic design as a career path for yourself!

Graphical designers are professionals who can communicate their ideas with their audience through visual means. These visual impacts are created using computer software. A graphical designer can work for website pages, logos, magazines, books, posters, digital games. Considering the work profile, graphic designers have to be very creative, innovative thinkers and should have good knowledge about the industry they are working in. 

Example: Akshar Pathak, Binoy Sarkar, Dashrath Patel, Gopi Prassana, Madhukar B Raju, R. K. Joshi, Sujata Keshavan, and Vikas Satwalekar are few famous Indian graphic designers. 

What are the roles and responsibilities for Graphical Designer?

  • Develop graphics and layouts for product illustrations, company logos, and web sites. 
  • Determine size and arrangement of illustrative material and copy and select style and size of type. 
  • Confer with clients to discuss and determine layout design. 
  • Create designs, concepts, and sample layouts, based on knowledge of layout principles and aesthetic design concepts. 
  • Use computer software to generate new images. 
  • Review final layouts and suggest improvements as needed. 
  • Maintain archive of images, photos, or previous work products. 
  • Prepare illustrations or rough sketches of material. 
  • Discuss the designs with clients or supervisors and make necessary changes. 
  • Draw and print charts, graphs, illustrations, and other artwork, using a computer. 
  • Enter critical information into computer equipment to create layouts for client or supervisor. 
  • Research about new software or design concepts. 
  • Study illustrations and photographs to plan the presentation of materials, products and services. 
  • Mark up, paste, and assemble final layouts to prepare layouts for printer.
